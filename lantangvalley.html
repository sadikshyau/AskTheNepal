Langtang Valley Trek – Himalayan Beauty Close to Kathmandu

Trip Introduction

The Langtang Valley Trek offers a stunning Himalayan experience without long travel days. Located just north of Kathmandu, the Langtang region is known for tranquil valleys, snow-capped peaks, rhododendron forests, glaciers, and rich Tamang culture. It’s perfect for trekkers seeking scenic beauty with fewer crowds.

Key Trip Facts

  • Trip Duration: 10–12 Days
  • Highest Elevation: 4,984 m (Kyangjin Ri)
  • Trek Difficulty: Moderate to Challenging
  • Best Seasons: March–May & September–November
  • Start / End Point: Kathmandu
  • Accommodation: Tea houses & guesthouses
  • Trek Style: Supported lodge trekking

Why Langtang Valley?

  • Short drive from Kathmandu
  • Stunning valleys, rivers, and glaciers
  • Authentic Tamang culture and monasteries
  • Rhododendron forests and alpine scenery
  • Less crowded trekking trails

What You’ll Experience

From forested trails to alpine plateaus near Kyangjin Gompa, the landscape changes dramatically. Peaks like Langtang Lirung, Dorje Lakpa, and Gang Chhenpo surround the valley, while prayer flags and mani walls reflect deep-rooted Buddhist culture.

Detailed Itinerary

Day 1 — Arrival in Kathmandu

Arrival, hotel transfer, rest, and trek briefing.

Day 2 — Drive to Syabrubesi

Scenic drive through hills, rivers, and villages.

Day 3 — Syabrubesi to Lama Hotel

Trek along Langtang River through forests and waterfalls. (5–6 hrs)

Day 4 — Lama Hotel to Langtang Village

Gradual climb through stone villages with valley views. (5 hrs)

Day 5 — Langtang Village to Kyangjin Gompa

Panoramic Himalayan scenery and glacial landscapes. (4–5 hrs)

Day 6 — Rest & Explore Kyangjin

Optional hike to Kyangjin Ri (4,984 m) for breathtaking views.

Day 7 — Kyangjin Gompa to Lama Hotel

Descend through forests and river valleys. (6 hrs)

Day 8 — Lama Hotel to Dhunche

Trail transitions from alpine terrain to foothills. (6 hrs)

Day 9 — Drive Back to Kathmandu

Scenic drive through rivers, farms, and hill towns.

Day 10 — Departure / Free Day

Airport transfer or optional Kathmandu sightseeing.

Inclusions & Support

  • Experienced local trekking guide
  • Porters for luggage
  • Tea-house accommodation
  • All meals during trek
  • Ground transportation
  • Langtang National Park permits

Trekking Tips

  • Take acclimatization seriously
  • Carry enough cash (limited ATMs)
  • Wear proper trekking boots and layers
  • Be prepared for sudden weather changes

Who Is This Trek For?

Perfect for trekkers seeking a shorter Himalayan adventure with rich culture, diverse landscapes, and fewer crowds — all close to Kathmandu.
